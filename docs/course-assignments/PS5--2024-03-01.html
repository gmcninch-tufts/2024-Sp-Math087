  3. In the notebook, we saw a probabilistic finite-state machine that described aging and population growth.

    A diagram of the finite-state machine can be constructed using the following code – PS5–FSM-code – or find it on colab: PS5–FSM-colab

    The matrix of interest is:

    \[A = \begin{bmatrix} f_0 & f_1 & f_2 & f_3 & f_4 \\ s_0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & s_1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & s_2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & s_3 & 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}.\]

    Suppose that the vector \(\mathbf{p}^{(t)} = \begin{bmatrix} p_0 & p_1 & p_2 & p_3 & p_4 \end{bmatrix}^T\) describes the population at a given time \(t\). Thus, for each time unit \(0 \le i \le 4\), the number of organisms of age \(i\) is equal to \(p_i\).

    We observed in the notebook that for \(j \ge 0\) the population \(j\) time units later is given by \[\mathbf{p}^{(t+j)} = A^j \mathbf{p}^{(0)}\] where \(A^j = A \cdot A \cdots A\) is the \(j\)-th power of the matrix \(A\).

    1. If the vector \(\mathbf{p} = \mathbf{p}^{(0)}\) represents the population at time \(0\), what part of the population at time \(j\) is described by the following product?

      \[\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \cdot A^j \cdot \mathbf{p}\]
